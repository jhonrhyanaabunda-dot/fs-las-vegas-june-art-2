## What cross-shoppers tell us at the desk

Three things show up in our Forester-versus-CR-V conversations more reliably than spec-sheet comparisons.

**Standard Symmetrical AWD is the deciding factor for buyers who actually need AWD.** Forester ships Symmetrical All-Wheel Drive standard on every trim. CR-V offers AWD as an option that adds roughly $1,500 to the trim's MSRP. Buyers who drive to Mount Charleston in winter, who take the Sunday loop through Red Rock when the monsoon hits, or who have a second home in Pahrump pick the Forester for the always-on engagement. The dollar comparison gets closer when you spec a CR-V with AWD, but the trim mix never gets you to the same value point as a base Forester Premium.

**Outward visibility wins more cross-shop conversations than the spec sheet suggests.** Forester's tall, square greenhouse with thin pillars gives meaningfully better outward visibility than the more sloped CR-V rooflines. Our Product Specialists have seen older buyers and family buyers consistently flag this in the test drive — the rear three-quarter visibility for changing lanes on I-15 is a measurable difference. CR-V buyers who flag interior quietness as their priority pick the Honda; Forester buyers who flag visibility pick the Subaru.

**EyeSight Driver Assist standard equipment matters at base trim.** Subaru ships EyeSight standard on every 2026 Forester trim — adaptive cruise control, lane centering, pre-collision braking, pre-collision throttle management. Honda Sensing is also strong on the CR-V. The difference at base trim is feature granularity: EyeSight has been on the market longer and the lane-centering tuning is well-regarded by independent reviewers. Buyers cross-shopping at the Premium and Sport trim level tend to flag this; buyers shopping at the top-trim level often find the two driver-assist suites comparable.

## How Las Vegas heat actually shows up in our service drive

We have inspected and serviced both Forester and CR-V trade-ins across full Mojave summer cycles. Three patterns repeat:

**Cooling system load handles 110°F+ on both vehicles when the maintenance is current.** Both manufacturers certify their cooling systems for desert-Southwest operation. The failure mode we have served is not the cooling system itself; it is owners who stretched coolant condition checks past the published interval. We have replaced coolant on both Foresters and CR-Vs at the 5-year mark and found additive depletion that should have been caught at year 3. Neither manufacturer publishes desert-specific testing data we can attribute, and we do not extrapolate from anonymous owner-forum threads.

**Hitch-mounted cargo capacity matters at the trail-head.** Both vehicles accept hitch-mounted bike racks. The 2026 Forester is rated at 1,500 lb tow on standard trims (3,000 lb on Wilderness); the CR-V's current tow rating is published at automobiles.honda.com. A typical hitch-mounted 4-bike rack with bikes loads roughly 200-300 lb, well within either vehicle's tongue rating. We have rotated tires on Foresters owned by Cottonwood Valley regulars and verified the rear suspension condition after years of bike-rack loading; the platform handles the load.

**Battery service runs 6 to 12 months earlier than the manuals predict.** Sustained 110°F+ underhood temperatures kill battery plates earlier than the published interval suggests. We have replaced batteries on both Outback and CR-V trade-ins at the 24-month mark when their original-region equivalents typically run 36 months. Have the battery load-tested every spring before the heat hits.

## Safety, technology, and warranty

Both suites are mature and feature-comparable at the standard-equipment level. The current 2026 IIHS designation for each vehicle (Top Safety Pick or Top Safety Pick+, by trim) is published at [iihs.org/ratings](https://www.iihs.org/ratings), and NHTSA five-star overall ratings have been awarded to recent model years of both vehicles. Verify the current 2026 NHTSA figure at [nhtsa.gov](https://www.nhtsa.gov).

Subaru EyeSight Driver Assist is standard on every 2026 Forester trim, including adaptive cruise control, lane centering, pre-collision braking, and pre-collision throttle management. Honda Sensing pairs a camera with millimeter-wave radar and is standard across the CR-V lineup. We have served buyers who walked in priorities-first on driver-assist; the Forester's stereo-camera tuning and the CR-V's radar-plus-camera architecture both perform well at urban speeds; the differences appear at the edges (low-light, heavy rain, lane-line ambiguity).

Both carry strong reliability histories. Consumer Reports publishes a model-year-specific predicted reliability score for each vehicle at [consumerreports.org](https://www.consumerreports.org/cars/) (subscription required for full score detail). Treat any single-year reliability rating as one data point alongside maintenance history and trim choice.

## Cost of ownership in Clark County

**Purchase price.** 2026 Forester Base starts at $29,995 (subaru.com); 2026 CR-V starting MSRP is published at [automobiles.honda.com/cr-v](https://automobiles.honda.com/cr-v).

**Fuel.** EPA-published fuel economy on the 2026 Forester runs 33 hwy / 26 city on Base and Premium gas trims; the 2026 Forester Hybrid lineup (Premium / Sport / Limited / Touring) is rated at 34 hwy / 35 city. CR-V combined MPG by trim is published at [fueleconomy.gov](https://www.fueleconomy.gov).

**Insurance.** Both are mainstream compact SUVs with strong safety scores and qualify for safety-equipment insurance discounts on most policies. Insurance cost in Clark County is driven by driver record and zip code more than by the Forester-vs-CR-V distinction.

**Resale.** Both hold residual value well in the segment. Current 5-year resale percentile data is published by [ALG](https://www.alg.com/) and [Kelley Blue Book](https://www.kbb.com/) for both 2026 vehicles.

**Maintenance.** Our Subaru service drive maintains the Forester under Subaru Express Service for routine work; CR-V owners typically use Honda dealers for the equivalent. Lincoln Complimentary-style first-N-years programs do not apply to either Forester or CR-V; both manufacturers run different maintenance-included structures.
